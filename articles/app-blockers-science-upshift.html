How To Effectively Use App Blockers: A Scientific Approach To Reducing Screen Time

Most users don't utilize app blockers the right way. They begin with overly strict restrictions and then quickly remove the blocking tool due to frustration. The radical approach rarely works. Fortunately, there's a better method.

Try this strategy instead:

  1. Block distracting apps for the entire day.
  2. Unblock them for brief periods when necessary.
  3. Repeat this process.

This method changes how you use your phone. Instead of mindlessly opening apps and losing awareness of time, you consciously decide how long you'll use them. When the app gets blocked again, you have the choice of what to do next. You might unblock repeatedly—that's fine. This additional step of unblocking makes the apps less attractive, which reduces your urge to use them over time.

Your first goal is to keep the blocking tool installed. Once you feel comfortable with the current restrictions, gradually increase them. Repeat until you've regained control over your screen time.

Upshift is designed for exactly this purpose.

The Key Concept: Friction

Have you ever left a website because it loaded too slowly? That's friction. Any friction, such as a loading delay, can make you stop using an app. Even a 0.1-second delay in search results, repeated millions of times, can cost companies like Google billions.

Social media apps work hard to eliminate friction. The less friction, the longer you stay. Apps like TikTok have mastered this: they're so smooth, you easily lose track of time.

App blockers like Upshift do the opposite—they add friction. The blocking disrupts the smooth experience of social media. Those extra 5 seconds needed to unblock add up, and over time, it will reduce your screen time. That's the power of friction.

Principle To Follow: "If You Struggle To Keep The Restrictions Active, They Are Too Strict"

Some people search for a solution that would make it impossible to unblock apps. But the truth is, you can always find a way to bypass restrictions.

Permanently blocked apps on your iPhone? You might use another device, like a Mac, or even consider resetting your phone. Blocked adult content? You can still find similar content on social media like Instagram. The point is: there's always a loophole because you have freedom.

And with that freedom, breaking deeply ingrained habits is challenging, especially without gradual change. That's why rehab centers exist—to create a controlled environment that restricts freedom and helps overcome addiction. But in everyday life, you don't have such external barriers. You're in control, and going "cold turkey" often leads to withdrawal symptoms that are too difficult to resist.

So follow this principle: "If you struggle to keep the restrictions active, they are too strict." Start with changes that are manageable. Aim for progress, not perfection. Gradual adjustments will ultimately reshape your habits and help you regain control.

Zero-Willpower Solutions Don't Exist

A useful parallel to managing screen time is weight loss. Despite countless solutions promising effortless fixes—such as medications like Ozempic or surgical interventions—none fully eliminate personal responsibility. Even the most powerful treatments still require behavioral change or lifestyle adjustments.

This highlights a crucial truth: zero-willpower solutions for lasting personal change don't exist. Searching for a magic fix leads to frustration. Real improvement always involves some level of effort.

App Blockers Aren't A Magic Solution

App blockers are a tool, not a cure-all. They won't automatically solve your screen time problems without your effort. Instead, they're just one piece of a larger puzzle—part of a set of conscious, holistic decisions you need to make to improve your life.

For example, beyond using app blockers, you need to actively replace phone time with other activities. This may seem obvious, but it's often overlooked: your phone offers endless, highly engaging entertainment. If you have nothing more compelling to do, of course you'll keep reaching for it! Regular life rarely feels as exciting—phones are designed to be addictive.

The key is to make intentional choices about how you spend your time. A blocking tool can help set boundaries, but it won't make those life decisions for you.
